## What's happening

You have a task. You know what you need. But you're staring at the prompt box trying to figure out how to explain it -- what context to include, what to leave out, how specific to be. You either write too little and get a bad result, or write a wall of text trying to cover everything.

You don't need to figure out what the AI needs to know. The AI can figure that out.

## The kata

### Let it interview you

Next time you start a task, try this:

```prompt
I need to add form validation to the profile page. Before you start, ask me anything you need to know.
```

The AI will ask about your tech stack, existing patterns, specific requirements, edge cases -- things you might not have thought to mention. Answer the questions, and now it has better context than any prompt you would have written.

### Make it a habit

This works for anything:

```prompt
I need to fix a bug where the submit button doesn't work after the first click. Ask me questions before you start investigating.
```

```prompt
I want to refactor how we handle API errors. Interview me about how it works today before proposing anything.
```

```prompt
I need to write tests for the auth flow. What do you need to know before you start?
```

### Why this works

You know things about your project that you don't know you know. The AI's questions surface that implicit knowledge. "What happens when the session expires?" -- you know the answer, but you wouldn't have thought to include it in your prompt.

It also prevents the AI from making assumptions. Without questions, it guesses. With questions, it knows.

## What to sit with

The best prompt isn't the most detailed one. It's the one that starts a conversation. You don't need to be good at prompting. You just need to let the AI be good at asking.
