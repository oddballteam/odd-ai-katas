## What's happening

You had a clear idea of what you wanted. You typed it out. The response was... close, but not quite. So you said "no, more like this." And then "actually, can you change the tone." And then "that's still not what I meant."

Ten messages later, you're frustrated. The AI seems to understand each individual correction, but the result keeps drifting. Or it fixes one thing and breaks something else. You're spending more time directing than you would have spent just doing it yourself.

## Why this is a problem worth naming

This isn't a "bad AI" problem. This is a **communication shape** problem. Most people interact with AI the way they'd ask a coworker - iteratively, conversationally, refining as they go. That works fine for some tasks. But for others, it's the wrong shape entirely.

The issue is that each follow-up message adds ambiguity. The AI has to reconcile your new instruction with everything you said before. Your mental model of the output is evolving, but the AI doesn't know which of your ten messages represents your *actual* intent.

## The scenario

> You need to write a technical design doc for a new caching layer. You ask the AI to draft it. The first version is too vague. You ask for more detail. Now it's too long and includes things you didn't want. You say "shorter, and focus on the invalidation strategy." It drops the parts you liked. You try to recover them. Three more messages. You're now spending more time editing-by-conversation than you would writing from scratch.

## What to sit with

Before reaching for a solution, notice the pattern:

- At what point did you realize the conversation was going in circles?
- What was the gap between what you *said* and what you *meant*?
- Did you start with a clear picture of the output, or were you figuring it out as you went?
- Would it have helped to give an example of what "right" looks like before asking for a draft?

## The kata

Try this: take a real task you'd normally iterate on over many messages. Before you send a single prompt, spend 2–3 minutes writing down exactly what the output should look like. Not what you want the AI to *do* - what you want the *result* to be. Include an example if you can. Then send that as your first message and see what happens.

Notice the difference. Not just in the output quality, but in how it changes the shape of the conversation that follows.
