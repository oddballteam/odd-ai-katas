## Bigger windows, same problem

Sonnet 4.5 at 1M tokens retrieves accurately only ~18% of the time. GPT-class models degrade similarly. The "lost in the middle" effect means things buried in a long history get less attention than the top and bottom. Bigger context windows move the cliff out, they don't remove it.

Treat anything past ~30-40% of the window as degraded territory. Once you notice the drop, do something about it.

## Signs your context has gone stale

- It repeats a suggestion you already tried.
- It re-reads a file it read ten minutes ago.
- It agrees with something that contradicts what you said earlier.
- It reintroduces a bug you just fixed.
- Answers get shorter, vaguer, or more generic.

When any of these show up, stop correcting and reset.

## Clear vs compact

- **Clear** (`/clear`, "New Chat") wipes the conversation. Fast, cheap, clean. Use when your next task is unrelated, or when the thread is poisoned.
- **Compact** (`/compact [focus]` in Claude Code, `/compress` in Gemini CLI) summarizes the history into a shorter form so you can keep going. Use mid-task when continuity matters. Steer the summary: `/compact focus on the auth refactor`. Don't wait for auto-compact at ~80% full; a proactive compact gives you a better summary.

## Plan, then reset

The highest-leverage move for a hard task: use plan mode to produce a concrete plan without writing code, save it as `HANDOFF.md`, clear, and start a fresh session that reads only the handoff. A clean session with a precise plan beats a long session with accumulated corrections.

- **Claude Code**: `/plan` or Shift+Tab.
- **Cursor**: Plan tab.
- **Codex CLI**: `/plan`.

Handoff prompt: "Write `HANDOFF.md`: what you tried, what worked, what didn't, and next steps. The next agent loads only this file."

## Commits as free context

Every commit is a natural checkpoint the AI can read cheaply. Instead of re-explaining a change from earlier, ask: "Read the last five commits and tell me where we left off." Commit small and often so the diffs stay useful. `gh`, `git log`, and `git diff` are the cheapest, highest-signal context sources you have.

## Exercise

1. Next time the AI repeats a suggestion it already tried, stop correcting. Say "Write HANDOFF.md with what we tried and what's next." Then `/clear` and paste the handoff into a new session.
2. On your next long session, commit whenever you finish a logical chunk. Tomorrow, open a new session and start with "Read the last 5 commits to get caught up." Notice how much less you have to explain.
