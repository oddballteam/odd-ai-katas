## Core concepts

| Term | What it means |
|------|--------------|
| **Token** | The unit AI reads and writes in. Roughly 3/4 of a word. Everything costs tokens. |
| **Context window** | How much the AI can hold in memory at once. When it fills up, older stuff gets dropped. |
| **Prompt** | Everything sent to the model, not just what you typed. Includes system instructions, files, and history. |
| **System prompt** | Hidden instructions your tool sends before you say anything. Your CLAUDE.md lives here. |
| **Model** | The specific AI brain (Opus, Sonnet, GPT-4). Different speed, cost, and smarts. You can usually switch. |

## How your tool works

| Term | What it means |
|------|--------------|
| **Agent** | An AI that takes actions: reads files, runs tests, edits code. Most coding tools are agents now. |
| **MCP** | Model Context Protocol. A plug-in system that connects your AI to external services like GitHub or Slack. |
| **Memory** | What your tool remembers across sessions. Preferences, facts about you, project context. |
| **Temperature** | Controls randomness. Low = predictable code. High = creative writing. Coding tools keep it low. |

## Things to watch for

| Term | What it means |
|------|--------------|
| **Hallucination** | When the AI confidently makes something up. A fake function, a wrong API. Normal, not a bug. Verify. |
| **RAG** | Retrieval-Augmented Generation. The AI searches real docs before answering instead of guessing from memory. |
| **Context engineering** | The practice of controlling what information the AI sees so it gives better answers. |
