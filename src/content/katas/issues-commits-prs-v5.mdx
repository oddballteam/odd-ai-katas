## Let the AI interview you

You probably can't recite your own commit format on the spot. But you'll recognize it when you see it. So instead of writing a skill from scratch, have the AI walk you through it.

The same pattern works for all three artifacts: commits, PRs, issues. Two prompts each. First one builds the skill. Second one wires it in.

## Example prompts

### Commits

```text
I want a /commit skill that knows my format. Interview me with one question at a time. When done, save the skill.
```

```text
Show me a hook that nudges the AI to use my /commit skill before any git commit.
```

### Pull requests

```text
I want a /pr skill that always opens PRs as drafts and uses my team's section structure. Interview me one question at a time, then save it.
```

```text
Show me a hook so the AI uses /pr instead of running gh pr create on its own.
```

### Issues

```text
I want an /issue skill that creates tickets on my board with the right labels and required fields. Interview me one question at a time, then save the skill.
```

## What good interview questions look like

A good interview prompt doesn't dump 12 questions on you. It asks:

1. Where do these get stored? (~/.claude/skills/?)
2. What's a recent example I could pattern off?
3. One specific question, wait for answer, next question.

If the AI starts writing the skill before asking enough questions, tell it to slow down.

## Exercise

1. Pick one of the three interview prompts above.
2. Answer the questions one at a time.
3. Check the resulting skill file. Is it short enough that you'd actually maintain it?
